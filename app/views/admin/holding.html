What a design history is

A design history is like a blog. It features posts that describe the development of new features, iterations of existing ones, or findings from user research. It will contain anything that may be useful to return to later.

Over time, a design history becomes a record of your designs. It tells the story of what you designed and why.

Why you should keep a design history

Keeping a design history makes it easy to know why a service is the way it is.

As services age, as they’re iterated, built and rebuilt, and team members come and go, institutional memory fades and questions about why a service is the way it is become hard to answer. Context gets lost, research disappears and the design becomes opaque.
